The Problem

When the air between the fins
stops moving, the device fails.

Passive cooling
No fans. No pumps. Natural buoyancy carries heat away — until it doesn't.
The dangerous limit
As fins narrow, boundary layers merge, velocity → 0, and natural convection is extinguished.
At what geometric scale does natural convection cease — and does shape change that answer?
Gap & Claim

Geometry is a spatial operator —
not a surface area provider.

the_gap.txt
What the literature never did
Every landmark study — Elenbaas, Bar-Cohen, Churchill-Chu, Kirinčić — treats the fin as flat, rectangular, surface-providing. None derives how the angled faces of a triangular cross-section in the plan view, or the curvature of a cylinder, modify the conservation equations.
the_claim.txt
What this thesis proves
Shape is a spatial operator on the governing equations — provable through the Péclet number and confirmed thermodynamically through the Bejan number. Shape decides whether heat is removed by convection — not just how much.

Movement 2 — the mathematical explanation

Pe = 1 ↔ Nu = 1 — derived, not fitted.

Péclet number · Eq. 12
Pe = w · L / α
Advection vs. diffusion. Is the fluid moving fast enough to carry heat, or is heat merely spreading through still air?
Transition velocity at Pe = 1
wtrans = α / L
= 2.044 × 10⁻³ m/s
Below this speed, the fluid carries no more heat than if it were perfectly still.
Nu minimum — first-principles
hmin = ρ cp wtrans
= 2.42 W/m²·K
Numin = 1.00
Pe = 1 maps exactly to Nu = 1. The point of convective extinction.
Pe > 1 → convection is active. Pe < 1 → diffusion dominates — the air might as well be standing still.

Mechanism — the triangle

The triangle deflects flow laterally.

plan_view_lateral_deflection.svg
Three angled faces · non-parallel boundaries.
Flow is continuously redirected in the horizontal (x, y) plane.
3D continuity equation
∂u/∂x + ∂v/∂y + ∂w/∂z = 0
Lateral velocity components u and v remain non-zero — sustaining the velocity field that the rectangular geometry's parallel walls would force to zero.
The mechanism is lateral, not vertical
The triangular pin is a prism: cross-section triangular in the plan view, side profile rectangular with constant height. There is no vertical tapering. The angled faces deflect rising buoyancy-driven air laterally, generating persistent u, v ≠ 0 that prevents the symmetric boundary-layer merging 2δt = S seen in rectangular channels.
Pe stays above 3.5 across all four triangular cases — even at Ra ≈ 3,700.
Mechanism — the circle

The circle dilutes viscous drag azimuthally.

circular_pin_top_view.svg
CFD · ParaView
Coolant volume grows ∝ 2π r.
Plumes around adjacent pins cannot merge.
Cylindrical Laplacian
∇² w → (1/r) ∂(r ∂w/∂r)/∂r
The 1/r factor mathematically dilutes viscous drag — resistance falls with distance from the surface.
Azimuthal pressure gradient
Curvature creates a favorable pressure gradient on the upstream side, thinning the boundary layer and increasing local heat flux.
Pe degrades smoothly from 4.15 → 3.60 — but never approaches extinction.
Movement 3 — the thermodynamic proof

The Bejan number is the
thermodynamic mirror of Pe.

Two sources of irreversibility
Ṡtotal = Ṡthermal + Ṡviscous
Thermal: productive — the cost of moving heat. Viscous: wasteful — energy lost to friction.
Bejan number
Be = Pe / (Pe + 1)
Partitions thermal vs. viscous entropy generation. Derived from the energy equation.
Thermodynamic criterion
Be > 0.5 · productive
Be < 0.5 · failed
Above 0.5: heat-transfer dominates. Below 0.5: viscous dissipation eats the exergy budget.
Pe ≫ 1 → Be → 1 (advection dominates).    Pe ≪ 1 → Be → 0 (diffusion takes over).

Appendix · The Foundation

The energy equation —
where the Péclet number begins.

Steady-state energy equation · fluid domain
ρ cp (u · ∇T) = k ∇²T + Φ
Advection of thermal energy by the flow equals molecular diffusion plus viscous dissipation. The first term is what convection does; the second is what stagnant air would still do.
Order-of-magnitude balance
advection ⁄ diffusion ≈ w · L ⁄ α = Pe
Comparing the two transport terms by their characteristic scales gives the Péclet number directly — no empirical fitting required.
Symbols
ρfluid density (kg/m³) cpspecific heat at constant pressure uvelocity vector field Ttemperature field kthermal conductivity (W/m·K) Φviscous dissipation function αthermal diffusivity = k / (ρ cp) w, Lcharacteristic velocity and length
Why this matters
When the Péclet number falls below unity, the advection term u · ∇T vanishes and the equation reduces to pure conduction k ∇²T = 0. The fluid stops carrying heat. Geometry is what determines the magnitude of u — and therefore whether convection lives or dies.
Appendix · Governing Equations

What the solver actually solves.

governing_eqs.md
Continuity · incompressible
∇ · u = 0
Mass conservation. What enters the domain must leave.
Momentum · Navier-Stokes + Boussinesq
ρ(u·∇u) = −∇p + μ∇²u
+ ρg β(T − T∞)
Inertia balances pressure, viscous, and buoyancy forces.
Energy · steady-state
ρ cp (u·∇T) = k∇²T + Φ
Advection balances diffusion plus viscous dissipation.
Boussinesq approximation: density variations are confined to the buoyancy term. Valid when ΔT/T∞ ≪ 1 — here (63.54 − 26.85)/300 ≈ 0.12. Ra = 10³–10⁴ keeps the flow laminar, so no turbulence model is required.
Appendix · Why narrow gaps choke

Viscous drag scales as 1/S².

Vertical momentum balance
μ ∂²w/∂x² ~ g ρ β ΔT
Viscous term on the left balances the buoyancy source on the right.
Scaling with gap width S
∂²w/∂x² ≈ w / S²
The velocity gradient inside a narrow channel grows inversely with the square of the gap.
Maximum velocity at the choking condition
wmax = ρ g β ΔT S² / 8μ
As S → 0, wmax → 0. The Pe = w·L/α follows and collapses toward zero. This is the analytical signature of convective extinction in a parallel-plate channel — why the rectangular 4 mm case lands at Pe = 0.156 and Nu = 1.73.

Appendix · The Biot Number

Bi ≈ 10⁻⁵ — physics lives in the fluid.

Biot number definition
Bi = h · Lc / ks
Ratio of external convective resistance to internal conductive resistance through the solid.
All 12 cases
Bi = 2.4 × 10⁻⁵  to  1.7 × 10⁻⁴
Ratio of aluminum's ks = 205 W/m·K to air's convective h ≈ 5–10 W/m²·K is ~4 orders of magnitude. The solid is effectively isothermal.
Consequence: geometry cannot improve performance by reshaping internal conduction. It can only reshape the external flow field — which is exactly what the Pe analysis measures.

Appendix · Where Bejan's equation comes from

Entropy generation — derivation chain.

Thermal irreversibility
Ṡth = Qconv·
(1/T∞ − 1/Tbase)
Exact Second-Law cost of moving heat across a finite ΔT.
Viscous irreversibility
Ṡvisc = ∫ (Φ / T) dV
Integrated viscous dissipation — energy destroyed by friction without transferring heat.
Bejan number identity
Be = Ṡth / (Ṡth + Ṡvisc)
= Pe / (Pe + 1)
The Pe → Be correspondence follows from the energy equation's advection/diffusion ratio. Derived, not postulated.
EGM principle (Bejan 1982, 1996): the thermodynamically optimal geometry minimizes Ṡtotal. For the 2 mm triangular pin, Ṡtotal = 8.662 × 10⁻⁴ W/K — the global minimum across the 12 cases.
Appendix · Why circular plumes don't merge

Coolant volume grows as 2π r.

Cylindrical Laplacian · dilution of drag
∇² w → (1/r) ∂(r ∂w/∂r)/∂r + …
Viscous resistance decreases with distance from the pin surface.
Radial coolant supply
Vcoolant(r) ∝ 2π r
Every additional radius brings linearly more cool air into contact with the plume.
Azimuthal pressure gradient
∂p/∂θ < 0 · upstream face
Bernoulli-buoyancy coupling thins δt and boosts local heat flux.
Thermal gradient never vanishes
∂T/∂r > 0 · for all r
Increasing coolant supply preserves the radial driving force — convection cannot extinguish.
This is the analytical reason circular Pe degrades smoothly (4.15 → 3.60) but never approaches 1.

Appendix · Anticipated Panel Questions

Why laminar. Why natural. Why RANS-less.

Q: Why laminar?
The Ra band is the answer
Ra = 2.38×10³ – 4.2×10³ sits orders of magnitude below the laminar-turbulent transition at Ra ≈ 10⁹ for vertical plates. Any turbulence model would add spurious diffusion — a modeling error, not a refinement.
Q: Why not forced convection?
Isolates geometric physics
Forced convection imposes an external velocity scale that masks the geometry-dependent buoyancy physics. Natural convection is the correct regime to isolate how shape acts as a spatial operator — the velocity field is entirely self-generated.
Q: Why not DNS / LES?
Steady-state is sufficient
At these Ra, the flow is steady and laminar — there are no turbulent eddies to resolve. The SIMPLE algorithm with second-order discretization and 2.3 M cells converges to grid-independent Nu values that match experiment within 7%.
